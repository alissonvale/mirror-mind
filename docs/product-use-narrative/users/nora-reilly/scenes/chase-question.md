# The Chase question

**Voice:** persona
**Journey:** the-crush
**Personas:** reader

## Briefing

Chase Reynolds. Junior. About to be my managing editor in August. I have a crush on him I've been treating as a logistical inconvenience.

This is the scene where I let the question be a feeling and not a problem to solve. What did I notice this week? What do I want to do about it (probably still nothing, but checked honestly each time, not by default)? Where is the line between professional respect and avoidance, and am I currently honoring one while pretending the other doesn't exist?

The mirror doesn't tell me what to do. It keeps me from outsourcing the thinking to the easier answer (just keep it professional) without checking whether that's actually the answer or just the safer one.
