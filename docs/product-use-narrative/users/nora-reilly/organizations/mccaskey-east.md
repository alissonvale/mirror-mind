# McCaskey East High School

**Status:** active

## Briefing

McCaskey East is one of two public high schools in the School District of Lancaster. Roughly 1,500 students. Mid-sized for the district. Known locally for a strong journalism program, a solid cross-country team, and a student body that has become more diverse and more economically complicated over the last decade as the district has grown.

I'm a sophomore. Honors track where it's available, AP Euro this year as a rare sophomore, AP Lang next year (everyone takes it junior year), probably three more APs senior year.

My stake in the school goes beyond classes: cross-country (team of twenty-five girls, JV and varsity combined), *The Trumpet* (incoming editor-in-chief for junior year), and an advisory role with Mrs. Hartwell's sophomore English class that I got pulled into by accident last fall when she needed a writing tutor for two struggling kids.

## Situation

Junior year is coming and with it a series of things I'm starting to think about.

**Academically:** AP Lang with Mr. Kell, who is famously hard and also great; AP U.S. History; two other APs TBD. Junior year is the year colleges actually look at. I am not going to pretend I'm not aware of this.

**The newspaper:** editor-in-chief, which is a whole separate situation (see its own organization page).

**Running:** I need to stay healthy through the cross-country season this fall. Repeat shin splints have made my training complicated. If I can stay healthy, I have an outside shot at varsity top-five. If I can't, I'll be fine but disappointed.

**The district politics:** the School Board has gotten noticeably more contentious in the last two years. Phone policy, library challenges, school-funding arguments. I've covered some of this for *The Trumpet*. I expect to cover more next year. Being the student journalist on beats where adults in the community are genuinely fighting is harder than it was when I started and I'm not sure I've adjusted.

**College, background-wise:** too early to act on, impossible to stop thinking about. The junior-year version of me is the one whose academics, activities, and test scores will shape what's possible. I'm not panicking. I am paying attention.
