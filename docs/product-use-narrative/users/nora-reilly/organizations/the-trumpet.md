# *The Trumpet*

**Status:** active

## Briefing

*The Trumpet* is McCaskey East's student newspaper, published monthly in print with a companion website. Staff of twelve in the current configuration: one EIC, one managing editor, one copy desk chief, three section editors (news, features, opinion), and six staff writers and photographers. Adviser: Ms. Pratt, who has run the paper for eleven years.

I joined as a freshman. I was a staff writer for my freshman year, assistant editor this year as a sophomore. I was elected editor-in-chief for my junior year in a staff vote last month — the first time in recent memory a sophomore has been elected into the role over a rising senior.

My summer task, ostensibly, is to think about the staff configuration, the editorial calendar, and the way production runs. My actual task, which I'm less clear about, is to figure out how to be the editor-in-chief of a group that includes people older than me who didn't all vote for me.

## Situation

Three live things:

**Jared.** The outgoing managing editor, a senior who graduates in June. He was the expected EIC before the staff vote went the way it did. He has been cordial since the vote. He has also been cold. We had one production Saturday together after the vote and the energy was not great. In two weeks he's gone, which is either a relief or a problem depending on how I handle the last two issues with him.

**The fall team.** I'm going to have to decide on the new staff configuration by mid-August. The obvious moves: promote Chase to managing editor (he's ready, he's the best writer and editor on staff beside me), promote Maya to news editor (she's a rising sophomore but she's the strongest news writer on the paper), and figure out the copy desk. The less-obvious question is whether to expand the staff — maybe two additional staff writers — and how to recruit them.

**The paper's editorial identity.** Under Jared and the outgoing EIC, *The Trumpet* has been competent and careful. I want it to be sharper. I want to run longer features. I want the op-ed page to have actual arguments, not just inoffensive student voices. I am aware that "I want the paper to be sharper" is a thing every new editor-in-chief says, and I am trying to distinguish what I actually want from the role-appropriate ambition. Not sure how much distance there is yet between the two.

**The adviser relationship.** Ms. Pratt likes me. She respects my writing. She also does not know me as an editor yet, only as a staff writer. The authority relationship is about to shift. I need to figure out how to be the EIC without reading the role as one that requires me to distance myself from her as a mentor. She's an asset. I would be stupid to turn the role into a rebellion.
