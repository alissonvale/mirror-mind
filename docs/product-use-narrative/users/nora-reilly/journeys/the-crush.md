# The Crush

**Status:** active

## Briefing

Chase Reynolds. Junior, copy desk chief, will be managing editor next year when I'm EIC. We have been working side by side for four months at the copy desk. Professional throughout. I have a crush on him that I have been treating as a logistical inconvenience rather than as a feeling.

He is smart, dry, thorough, not performative. He writes cleanly. He handles other people on the staff with a kind of quiet patience that I notice. He has never said anything that could be read as flirtation. He has also never said anything that explicitly closed a door.

## Situation

The live question: do I do anything about it, or do I let it run its course.

What's true:

**The working relationship is going to intensify.** When I make him managing editor in August — which I am going to do, because he is the correct person for the role — we are going to be in a different relationship than the one we're in now. More meetings. More one-on-ones. More time together making decisions together.

**The crush is unlikely to fade on its own.** Four months in, it has not faded, and the intensification of the working relationship is going to test the "it'll just go away" theory in the direction of more, not less.

**I don't know if he feels anything.** My honest read is that he has never given me a signal either way. He is professional. He is also someone who is probably professional with most people he works with. That doesn't tell me anything. I am in the classic bind of not being able to interpret a neutral signal.

**The risk calculus, practically.** If I say something and he doesn't reciprocate, I have to run the paper with him next year with that conversation between us. That is bad. If I say nothing and he does reciprocate but doesn't say anything, I lose a potentially real thing to silence. That is also bad, if less definitively.

**The risk calculus, emotionally.** The real risk of saying something is not rejection. I can handle rejection. The real risk is that I'm wrong about him and I misread a kindness as interest, and I have to sit with that for a year. That embarrassment is the thing I'm actually afraid of.

**What I've been avoiding thinking about.** Whether the crush is specifically about him, or whether it is partly about the fact that I've been working closely with him at the copy desk and have therefore been paying him more attention than I pay other people. Proximity creates affection. I have not fully separated these.

**The friend I could talk to about this.** Amelia, historically. But Amelia and I are in a complicated place (see the friendship journey). I'm not sure she's the person I want to bring this to right now. Which is its own kind of loss.

**The thing I don't want to say out loud.** I want to know. I want to stop wondering. Whether the answer is yes or no matters less than having the question resolved. But "wanting to stop wondering" is not a great reason to initiate a conversation that will complicate my working life for a year. Or it might be a fine reason. I genuinely cannot tell yet.
