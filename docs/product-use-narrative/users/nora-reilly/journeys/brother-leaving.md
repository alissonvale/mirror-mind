# My Brother Leaving

**Status:** active

## Briefing

My brother leaves for college in August. He has been my brother for my entire life. He is now about to not live here. I do not yet know how to think about this, and I have been handling it by not thinking about it.

## Situation

What's actually true:

**I have not said anything about it to anyone.** Not to him. Not to my parents. Not to Amelia. The fact that I haven't said anything is itself information.

**He has not said anything to me either.** Which is normal for him. He doesn't initiate emotional conversations. I'm not upset about it. I would be suspicious if he did initiate one.

**My mother is handling it worse than she is letting on.** I see her watching him at dinner. I see her lingering in his doorway when he's out. She thinks she's hiding it. She isn't.

**My father is being steady.** Which is also what he does. He is, I think, more affected than he shows. He has not said anything either.

**The house is going to change.** Logistically: his room becomes the guest room. His chair at the dinner table sits empty. His car, which he shares with my mother, goes with him — which is not a small thing, because I am getting my license this summer and had started doing a calculation about whether I'd get occasional access. That calculation just stopped being relevant.

**The thing I have been quietly avoiding.** We used to just be the kids. It will now be one kid. That's a different kind of house, and a different shape of dinner, and a different set of expectations directed at me from my parents. I don't think they mean to do this, but they are going to — more attention, more concern, more interest in my running and my writing and my college prep. I do not know whether I will find that comforting or oppressive.

**What I haven't said to him.** That I am going to miss him. I am not sure I know how to say it, because he and I don't say those kinds of things to each other. We show it in other ways. But I am aware that "we don't say those things" is a habit the two of us built together, and there is a version of the summer where I say the thing once, and it sits between us without having to be said again. I think I would like to be the kind of person who says the thing once. I don't know if I will be.

**The shape of the summer.** I have cross-country training, the newspaper transition, a possible part-time job, and my brother being home for three more months. I am aware that the summer is finite. I am also aware that trying to make the summer "meaningful" is the most surefire way to guarantee it isn't.
