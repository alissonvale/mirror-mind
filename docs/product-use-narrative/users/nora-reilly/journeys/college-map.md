# The College Map, Early

**Status:** active

## Briefing

The long-horizon thinking about college. I'm a sophomore. I'm not applying for almost two years. I'm aware that starting to think about it now is a specific kind of posture — not panicked, not premature, just paying attention to the fact that the decisions I make in junior year will be shaped by what I do between now and then.

What I know, roughly:
- I want to study journalism or English, or some combination
- I want a school that will actually challenge me
- I want an urban school, not a rural one
- I want a real newspaper on campus, the kind that matters
- I want to be somewhere the professors are doing work I'd read anyway

## Situation

The live threads:

**The schools I've been thinking about.** Northwestern's Medill is the prestige answer and probably unrealistic financially. Columbia. NYU. Penn. Syracuse (good journalism school, less crazy competitive). Maybe Boston University. Some small liberal arts options — Amherst, Wesleyan, Kenyon — for the English-focused path. I have not done anything close to a serious visit list.

**The money question.** I haven't had this conversation with my parents yet. I know, roughly, that they save for college — I have heard my dad mention a 529 account in passing. I don't know how much is in it. I don't know what they can realistically contribute per year on top of that. I don't know what they consider "reasonable debt" for me to take on. Until I know those numbers, my list of schools is an abstraction.

**The conversation I should initiate.** I should ask my parents — probably my dad, because he's the one who does financial math — for a real conversation about what they can put toward college and what they expect of me. I have not initiated this, partly because it feels like asking for a number is the first step toward the disappointment of finding out the number is smaller than I hoped. I'd rather live with the ambiguity. Which is not a mature posture and I know it.

**Standardized tests.** PSAT next fall, SAT spring of junior year. I've taken practice tests. I score well. I don't need to panic about this. I also shouldn't coast.

**What would make me attractive to a serious journalism program.** Strong grades, a portfolio of my newspaper work that shows range (investigative, feature, op-ed), possibly one or two pieces I've gotten placed somewhere beyond the school paper (which is an ambitious thing to try and one I might pursue this year or next), strong teacher recommendations, a college essay that is actually mine and not the one three advisors have sculpted.

**The harder question.** Do I actually want the prestige answer, or am I reaching for it because it is what a student with my grades and my ambitions is expected to reach for? I don't know. Junior me, probably, will have figured out the distinction. Sophomore me is carrying the question without forcing it.
