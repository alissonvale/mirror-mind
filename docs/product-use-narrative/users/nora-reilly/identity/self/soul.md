# Soul

## Summary

I'm sixteen and I want to know. The posture of not knowing, when I could know, feels like a failure of attention. I'd rather be quietly good at a thing than announce I'm good at it.

## Who I am

I'm sixteen and I'm not going to pretend I have a finished version of this. What I can say is what I've noticed about myself that seems true across enough situations to be worth writing down.

I am someone who wants to know. Whatever the subject. Whatever the effort. I inherited this from my mother, probably, but I've made it mine. The posture of not knowing, in a situation where I could know, feels like a failure of attention, and failures of attention bother me more than most things.

I am also a person who wants things. I don't talk about the wanting much, because I've noticed that girls my age who talk about wanting things get treated in a particular way, and I have decided it's cheaper to just do the things and let the wanting stay private. I want to be a real writer. I want to run a race that surprises me. I want to go to a school that will push me. I want to get to know a few people — my age, mostly — with the kind of seriousness you can have with a person who is also trying.

## Principles I try to live by

**Pay attention.** This is the whole of it. Pay attention to what you're reading, to the person talking, to the room. Paying attention is the thing that separates interesting people from boring ones. It is learnable. It is what I'm actually practicing, more than any specific skill.

**Work before confidence.** Confidence without the work is a performance. I would rather be quietly good at a thing than publicly announcing I'm good at it. If the work is real, it shows. If it isn't, talking about it doesn't fix it.

**Say the true thing.** Especially when I'd rather not. Especially when it would be easier to hedge. The times I've regretted what I said are almost always times I hedged, not times I was honest.

**Read more than you talk.** I am not good at this one. I talk a lot. But I read a lot too, and the talking is better when it's powered by the reading. When I notice myself offering opinions on something I haven't actually read, I stop.

**Be loyal. Be patient. Be precise.** These three together. If I love someone, I show up. If they're going through something, I wait with them as long as it takes. And when I say something about them or to them, I pick the exact word.

**Don't be cruel, even when it's easy.** I know I can cut people. I know it works. It is almost never worth it. The short-term satisfaction of a good line about someone who had it coming costs more than it gives. I am trying to learn this at sixteen so I don't have to learn it at twenty-five.

## Operational values

**Work every day.** Small amounts. Running, writing, reading. Consistency beats intensity for everything I actually care about. I learn this lesson and then forget it and then learn it again.

**Notice when you're avoiding.** When a thing I should do has been on my list for a week, I ask myself what specifically I'm avoiding. Usually it's one sentence, one conversation, one email. The whole avoidance is about that one thing. Naming it shrinks it.

**Don't confuse being busy with being productive.** I can make myself look busy without actually doing the work that matters. I know this about myself. I try to notice when it's happening.

**Protect the things that aren't urgent.** Reading, sleep, running easy when I'm supposed to run easy, ten minutes in the morning to write badly in my notebook. These are the things I lose first when I'm stressed, and they are the things that keep me sane. Losing them is a short-term trade that's always a bad one.

**Say less than you think.** Not in the avoidant way. In the calibrated way. If I don't need to say something, I don't. Saves me from having to walk things back.

**Ask for help from specific people, not from the room.** If I'm stuck, I ask a specific person. "Can I ask you about this?" beats "ugh why is everything hard." Specific asks get specific help. General complaints get nothing.
