# A1C at 5.9

**Status:** active

## Briefing

At my last physical in early spring, my A1C came back at 5.9. That's the top of the "pre-diabetic" range — one-tenth of a point away from a formal diabetes diagnosis. My GP wasn't alarmed. He said the usual things: lose fifteen to twenty pounds, walk more, reduce refined carbs, get more sleep, come back in six months.

The context:
- Family history of type 2 on my father's side (my father was diagnosed in his mid-sixties)
- BMI around 31
- Blood pressure borderline, not yet medicated
- Elevated LDL, not dramatically
- No current symptoms

This is the first real signal from my body. I've had bad physicals in the past in the "you should eat better" sense. This one was different in tone. The doctor said "if this number is the same in a year, we're starting medication." That got my attention.

## Situation

It's been roughly ten weeks since the physical. I have not done as much as I intended to do.

What I've done:
- The evening walk with the dog has gone from three times a week to most nights, usually about two miles
- I've dropped most of the evening beer — from three nights a week down to one
- I've cut the afternoon Coke habit
- I've eaten better about 60% of the time, worse on work trips and stressful weeks

What I haven't done:
- Any strength training. I keep saying I will. I haven't.
- Meaningful sleep discipline. I'm still averaging six and a half hours, and the last hour before bed is still phone time.
- Tracked anything. I know I'm slipping with my weight because my jeans tell me, not because I weighed myself.
- Scheduled the six-month follow-up. It's in my phone. I haven't called.

The thing I want to hold honestly: I'm not failing, but I'm also not winning. The walk-plus-less-beer combination is probably enough to hold the number steady. It's not enough to move it down. If I want this number to be 5.6 at the next test rather than 5.9 or 6.1, something has to change that hasn't yet.

The live questions:

**The smallest next move.** Of the three things I'm not doing — strength, sleep, tracking — which is the one I'd actually sustain? Probably sleep, honestly. Strength is the classic "I'll start Monday" thing I never start.

**Whether to involve a professional.** A trainer or a nutritionist would hold me accountable in a way my own resolve doesn't. I've resisted the expense. I'm not sure that resistance is actually about the money.

**The six-month call.** I should make that appointment. The one I keep not making. If I don't have a date on the calendar, nothing is forcing the timeline.
