# After the call with Mom

**Voice:** persona
**Journey:** mothers-care
**Personas:** son
**Length:** brief

## Briefing

Mom is in her late seventies, alone in the house since Dad died, declining a little faster than she lets me see. We talk on Sundays. After the call I'm always sitting with something — what she said, what she didn't say, what the silence around a particular topic meant.

This is the scene where I try to hear the call again with the gain turned up. What did she actually need from me today? What did I duck? What's the next phone call going to ask of me that I'm not yet ready to give?

Not for solutions. For honesty about where the slope is, and how I'm pacing my response to it.
