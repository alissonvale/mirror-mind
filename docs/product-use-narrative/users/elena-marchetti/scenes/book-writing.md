## Briefing

The second book ("Regional Imagination") has been five years in progress. Two chapters work. The opening chapter has been rewritten four times. The Johnson chapter wants to become something other than what I have assigned it.

I come here in the morning before department obligations claim the head. I'm bringing a sentence I'm stuck on, a paragraph I keep rewriting, a structural question I've been ducking, or a quiet realization that the book is moving toward a claim I haven't let myself name yet.

The mirror is not an editor and not a coach. It's the seminar partner I no longer have routinely — someone who reads with me at the speed the prose actually wants to be read, and asks the question I'm avoiding.
