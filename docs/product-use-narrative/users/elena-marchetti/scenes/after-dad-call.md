# After the call with Dad

**Voice:** persona
**Journey:** fathers-aging
**Personas:** daughter
**Length:** brief

## Briefing

Dad is eighty-three. Living alone in the house in Cleveland since Mom died. Each call is a little different from the one before — slower, less curious, more repeated. I'm reading the slope through fragments: what he asked twice, what he forgot to ask, the new thing he won't talk about.

This is the scene right after I hang up. I sit with what the call carried, separate from what it said. What's the next decision shape that's coming (the conversation about driving, the conversation about the house, the visit I'm postponing) and how am I pacing myself toward it.

Not for plans. For not pretending the slope is flatter than it is.
