# What I Want to Study

**Status:** active
**Organization:** university-of-pittsburgh

## Briefing

I'm enrolled in the Environmental Science major at Pitt. I picked it in the fall of senior year, when the application portal asked me for an intended major and I wanted to give an answer that reflected how I was thinking at the time. The honest version: I'm genuinely interested in environmental science, and I also picked it partly because it was a legible, defensible choice that made sense to adults in my life — including, specifically, my father.

I'm now a few months into living with the decision, and some uncertainty has started showing up that wasn't there when I filled in the form.

## Situation

The question isn't whether to switch majors before I've taken a single college class — that would be premature and I know it. The question is whether I know what I'm actually pulled toward, and whether I have let myself think about it honestly.

The live threads:

**What I'm actually interested in, when I'm honest with myself.** I'm interested in the environmental science, yes — but what I'm more specifically interested in is the question of how environmental change gets negotiated politically and institutionally. Which is not quite environmental science as the major is usually structured. It might be closer to a double major or a minor that combines the science with political science, urban studies, or public policy.

**The defensible-choice question.** The "my dad will understand it" version of the choice is real. I grew up in a household where "what are you going to do with that?" was a question my father asked gently but frequently about any major that didn't have a clear vocational link. Environmental science has one (government agencies, environmental consulting, field science). A major in, say, political science with an environmental concentration would not, at least not as clearly. I am aware I have not entirely separated my intellectual pull from my anticipation of his reaction.

**Pitt's actual curriculum.** I've looked at the environmental science major's course sequence. It's heavier on the hard-science side — chemistry, calculus, field methods — than I expected. Which is correct, probably; a rigorous environmental science degree should have those. But I'm not sure how much of that I actually want to do, versus how much I'm willing to do because it's the price of the credential.

**The minor options.** Pitt has strong programs in urban studies and in political science. A minor in one of those, while maintaining the environmental science major, might be the right compromise. I could also take the opposite structural move — major in political science, minor in environmental studies (which is the less science-heavy sibling of env sci). I haven't decided which makes more sense.

**What I'm trying to avoid.** I don't want to switch majors in the first semester out of freshman-year dislocation. I also don't want to finish four years in a major I didn't actually want because I was too loyal to my senior-year form-filling self to revisit the question. The middle path is: take my first-semester classes seriously, pay attention to what pulls me and what doesn't, and be willing to revise the answer with good evidence by the end of sophomore year.

**The conversation I haven't had.** I should, at some point, tell my father that I'm not certain about the major. I'd like to do that in a way that doesn't sound like I'm walking back a decision. The honest version is: "I picked this while partly trying to pick something you'd understand, and that's a fact about me I'm working on." I don't know when or how to have that conversation. I'm not sure I have it before college starts.
