# The Breakup

**Status:** active

## Briefing

Emma and I dated for ten months. We broke up in early February, three months ago as I'm writing this. It was mutual in name and not quite mutual in feeling — she said the words. I understood why. We had grown apart in a way neither of us was fighting, and it was better to end it than to force something neither of us was sure we wanted anymore. None of that part is complicated.

What's complicated is that I am still thinking about her more than I expected to be, three months in. I notice it most at six o'clock, because that was the hour we used to text about the day. I notice it sometimes when I read something I would have wanted to tell her about. I notice it in the way I have not been interested in anyone else, which I originally thought was because it was too soon and am now starting to suspect is because it's specifically her I'm still processing.

## Situation

I am not in acute pain. I am also not over it, and I have been telling myself I am closer to over it than I actually am.

What is real:

**The six o'clock thing.** It happens most days. It's not a crisis — it lasts maybe two minutes, I notice it, and I move on. But it has not faded, and I thought it would have by now.

**The not-telling-my-parents thing.** I have not told my parents how much the breakup affected me. They know we broke up. They were generous in not making a thing of it. They did not ask follow-up questions, which I was grateful for at the time and now occasionally wish they had. I'm aware I could bring it up. I don't.

**The one friend I told.** I told Jake, once, in March, after a night out. He handled it well, which is to say he didn't make it a production, didn't push me to say more, and didn't bring it up again afterward. I've considered bringing it up with him again. I haven't.

**Seeing her at school.** It's fine. It's actually fine. We're cordial. We sit at different tables. It would be worse if we were avoiding each other; we aren't. But "fine" is not "nothing."

**The honest question.** I do not know whether I want to try to stay friends with her after graduation or whether that's a story I'm telling myself because the alternative — that I just don't see her again — feels too clean for something that was ten months of my life. She's going to Ohio State. I'm going to Pitt. The geographic answer will probably decide for us. But I'd like to at least know what I want, rather than letting the geography be the decision.

**The thing I'm avoiding thinking about.** I haven't let myself think about whether part of why I'm still carrying this is that I haven't yet been interested in anyone else. If I had been, I'd have more material to compare against, and "I still think about her" would have a clearer meaning. Without that comparison, I can't tell if I'm carrying her specifically or if I'm carrying the shape of what the relationship gave me. That distinction matters. I haven't worked it out.
