# Leaving Home

**Status:** active

## Briefing

The interior thread of the last few months: the slow realization that I am actually going to stop living in this house, this town, this life, and that I am not yet sure how I feel about any of that. Separate from the logistics of the college transition and the politics of the major question, this is the register of the thing itself.

I have lived in this house my entire life. Same bedroom. Same walk to the bus. Same dog (different dog, actually — the current one is two — but the shape of having a dog). Same kitchen on a Sunday morning. My sister has been my sister my entire life. My parents have been married my entire life. I don't have a contrast to compare this against. It's the only baseline I've ever had.

## Situation

I am moving through the last months of a version of my life, and I don't yet have the language for it.

What I actually notice:

**Small things getting heavy.** My mom making Sunday breakfast. My dad asking me how my speech went. The dog sleeping in my doorway. My sister putting on music I don't like and singing along badly. Ordinary moments that I am treating, against my will, as though they are finite, because they are.

**My mom.** She is handling my leaving worse than she is letting on. She is being careful about not putting it on me. I can see her being careful. I don't know whether to name it or let her handle it her own way. Either choice feels like it could be the wrong one.

**My dad.** He is being steady. That is what he does. He has not said anything about me leaving specifically, but I notice he is lingering a little longer at dinner. I also notice he is being extra careful to ask me about things I'm working on. He would describe this, if asked, as normal father behavior. I know him well enough to know it isn't.

**My sister.** Nora has not said anything to me about my leaving. I don't know if she's not thinking about it or is protecting herself. Either is a possibility. I don't know how to open the conversation without making it heavier than she wants it. I keep deciding to wait for her to open it, and she keeps not opening it. This is probably a mistake I'm making repeatedly.

**The house.** I have noticed myself, in the last month, looking at specific corners of the house in a way I haven't before. The spot on the kitchen floor where the tile has a chip. The window in the upstairs hallway that faces the neighbor's maple. The doorway of my own room, the exact angle of it. I am committing these to memory without having decided to. I don't know yet what to do with that.

**The summer.** I get three months in this house before I leave. I want to make them good. I am also aware that trying too hard to "make them good" is the kind of thing that guarantees they don't feel good in the moment. I'd rather just be in them and let them be what they are, which means not turning the summer into an archive project I'm performing for myself.

**What I'm not saying.** I love this house. I love my family. I am also, honestly, ready to live somewhere else. Those two things are not in contradiction, but they feel like they are in contradiction, and I have not yet worked out how to hold both of them at once without one of them feeling like a betrayal of the other.
