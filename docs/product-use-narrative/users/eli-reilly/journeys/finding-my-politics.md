# Finding My Politics

**Status:** active

## Briefing

The ongoing work of figuring out what I actually believe about politics, climate, labor, and the specific shape of American contradictions — separate from what I have repeated, what I have absorbed from friends and podcasts, and what I have performed at points when I wanted to sound certain.

I came into political awareness mostly through the climate frame, starting in middle school. That's widened in the last two years as I've worked with the coalition and read more seriously. I'm now far enough into the reading to know I'm nowhere near the bottom of it, and that's its own kind of weight.

## Situation

The thing I keep noticing is that my political vocabulary is more confident than my political understanding, and the gap between the two is where I get embarrassed.

The live threads:

**What I'm actually reading versus what I'm claiming to have read.** I am halfway through *The Dawn of Everything*. I have claimed, in conversation, to have views about it that are actually views from the book review I read. This is not a crisis — I'll finish the book — but I notice it, and I don't like it about myself, and I'd like to stop doing it.

**The electoralism question.** A friend argued with me last month that my skepticism about electoral politics is a luxury I can afford because of who I am demographically. She was not entirely wrong. I have not fully thought through my response. I'm in the middle of working out whether the position I held before the argument was actually mine or was borrowed from one of the podcasts I listen to — and if it was borrowed, whether I would still hold it if I were defending it from my own thinking.

**Identity and material.** I'm trying to work out how I think about the tension between identity-politics framings and materialist framings of the left. I have friends who are purists in either direction. Neither purist position seems right to me. I don't yet know whether that makes me a muddled centrist or an honest thinker working toward an integration. Some days one. Some days the other.

**The household.** My father is politically sympathetic to me, but we are not on the same page, and some of the gap is meaningful rather than decorative. He's a pragmatist to a degree I'm not. He treats organizing as noble but not, for him, necessary. I respect his position and I also don't fully accept it. I have not had the conversation with him in a way that would require either of us to actually defend what we believe. I'd like to, eventually, but not while I'm still living in the house. The stakes are different when you live with the person.

**My own limits.** I have told myself, repeatedly, that I am going to join Pitt DSA in the fall. I don't know if I actually will. Part of me wants to; part of me is afraid of being a nineteen-year-old kid in a room of grad students and adults who are going to see through my citations within fifteen minutes. That fear is not about my politics; it's about whether I've earned the right to hold them in a serious room. That's the thing I need to sit with more honestly than I have been.
