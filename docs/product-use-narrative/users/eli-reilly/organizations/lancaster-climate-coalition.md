# Lancaster Climate Action Coalition

**Status:** active

## Briefing

The Lancaster Climate Action Coalition is a small, volunteer-run local group that organizes around state energy policy (especially Pennsylvania's RGGI situation, which has been stuck in court for years), local zoning decisions that affect solar deployment and pollinator corridors, and occasional coordinated school-strike events with the regional Sunrise-adjacent networks.

Membership: about twenty-five consistent volunteers, another thirty or so who show up to specific events. Three-person steering committee that meets separately from the general meetings. No paid staff. Funded by small donations and occasional project grants.

I joined in the spring of my sophomore year, after a friend brought me to a school-strike planning meeting. I started by helping with table setup and social-media posting. Over the last two years I've grown into a role where the adult organizers ask my judgment on anything involving high school students — event design, messaging, how to handle school administrators who push back. Last month I gave my first actual speech at a rally. It was seven minutes long. It was the thing I spent the most emotional energy on all spring.

## Situation

My time with the coalition is about to change in a way nobody has named yet. I'm leaving Lancaster in August. The people who trained me are not going to replace me, exactly — there will be other high school students who come up — but my specific role is ending, and we haven't talked about a transition.

The live things on my mind:

**The transition conversation.** I should be initiating a conversation with the steering committee about who among the current high school members could step into the role I've been filling. There's one candidate I think would be good — a sophomore named Maya who has been showing up consistently and thinking well. I haven't talked to her about it or to the steering committee about it. I should, before the summer gets away from me.

**The summer work.** There are two events I've already committed to — a clean-energy town hall in June and a fair-in-the-park tabling event in July. I'll be there for both. After that, I'll probably taper, with the understanding that I'm still available remotely.

**Whether to stay connected from Pittsburgh.** The coalition does not meaningfully have a "remote member" model. A few people who moved away stay on the email list and occasionally show up when they're in town, but they're not part of the actual work. I'd like to stay more connected than that. I'm not sure if that's realistic or if it's a version of the college-freshman thing where you stay loyal to your high school community in a way that eventually starts pulling you away from the new thing you're supposed to be building.

**Pitt DSA and whatever else is there.** The coalition is where I learned what organizing actually is. Pittsburgh is a bigger city with a more developed political ecosystem. What I want from the fall is to find a chapter or a campaign or a tenant union that has some of the same seriousness. I don't yet know what that is. I have a list of names to look up when I arrive.
