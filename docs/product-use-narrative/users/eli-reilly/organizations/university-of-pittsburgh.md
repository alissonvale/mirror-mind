# University of Pittsburgh

**Status:** active

## Briefing

The University of Pittsburgh is where I'll be a freshman in the fall. I'm enrolled in the Kenneth P. Dietrich School of Arts and Sciences, planning to major in Environmental Science with what the advising letter calls a "strong interest in Environmental Studies minor or concentration." I got in early-round in December. I'll be living in Sutherland Hall, one of the upper-campus first-year dorms. I don't yet have a roommate assigned.

I chose Pitt for a combination of reasons: the environmental science program has real research faculty who do public-facing work, Pittsburgh is a place I have some small connection to (my mother's cousin lives there and I visited twice in high school), the financial aid package was workable, and — less articulated — the city itself appealed to me in a way I didn't fully know how to explain at the time of the decision.

I'll be three hours by car from home, two and a half with good traffic. I don't own a car. My plan for getting home for breaks is either the Megabus or a ride with one of the two other Lancaster-area kids I know are going to Pitt.

## Situation

I haven't started yet. That's the whole situation, and it's its own kind of weight.

The things I'm actively carrying about Pitt right now:

**Housing.** Sutherland is fine as a building; it's an upper-campus dorm with decent rooms and a long walk to most of my likely classes. The roommate lottery hasn't resolved. I'm mildly anxious about getting paired with someone whose politics or vibe is going to make the dorm room unusable as a place to think. I know this is a thing I can't control and am trying not to catastrophize.

**Orientation.** It's the second week of July, three days, mostly scripted. I know a couple of things about it from a friend who went last year. I'm not worried about the event itself. I'm worried about being in a room with several hundred strangers and feeling how much I miss being in a room with people I know.

**Coursework.** My first-semester schedule isn't finalized yet — it opens in July — but based on Dietrich's first-year advising, I'll probably be in the intro environmental science sequence, a required composition course, a general-ed math course, and one free elective. I've been thinking about what the free elective should be. I'm leaning toward something in political science or labor studies; I'm also aware that could be repeating a pattern of picking the "politics-signaling" elective rather than something that would genuinely expand me.

**Clubs and community.** I've been telling myself I'll join the Pitt DSA chapter, the birding club (it exists and is semi-active), and maybe one of the environmental advocacy groups. I haven't committed to any of this in a way that would survive a week of overwhelm. I'm aware that over-committing in the first month is a common mistake. I'm also aware that under-committing because of fear of over-committing is an inverse version of the same mistake.

**The cost.** My parents are paying a portion. I'm taking a modest federal loan each year. I have a summer job lined up at a local nature center that will cover my spending money. The money is workable. It's not something I'm anxious about in a material sense; it is something I'm aware of in the sense that I don't plan to be careless about it.
