**Assistant:**
Empid at Middle Creek on May 3, silent, in scrubby edge. Your options in Pennsylvania in that habitat on that date are basically: Least, Willow, Alder, Acadian, maybe a late Yellow-bellied if you got lucky. You ruled out Acadian implicitly — they stay in wet deciduous woodland, not scrubby edge. Yellow-bellied is usually gone by early May but straggles. Let's go through the field marks.

The teardrop-shaped eye ring is your best clue. That's classic Least. The complete, prominent, almost-goggle-y eye ring on Least is the first thing a lot of birders learn after they stop trying to ID silent empids generally.

Short primary projection — also consistent with Least. Willow and Alder have longer primary projections; Willow especially.

The faint yellow wash below is interesting. Least can show a faint yellow, especially early in spring; Willow and Alder are usually whiter below. Yellow-bellied would show much more yellow, and usually a bolder eye ring with a different shape. If the yellow was faint rather than strong, that's still in Least's range.

My read: probably Least Flycatcher. The eye ring is the strongest mark, and the other features don't push you off it.
